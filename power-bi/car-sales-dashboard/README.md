# Car Sales Analytics Dashboard

**Tool:** Power BI Desktop &nbsp;|&nbsp; **File:** `Car_sales.pbix`

## Problem

Dealership sales data was scattered across spreadsheets and legacy tools, with no unified,
real-time view of performance. Manual reporting was slow and made it hard to spot seasonal trends
or compare regions and manufacturers.

## What this dashboard does

A single interactive Power BI report consolidating transaction-level sales data into:
- An **executive KPI overview** (total sales, YoY growth, units sold, average price)
- A **weekly sales trend** view for spotting seasonality
- Breakdowns by **body style, color, region, and manufacturer**
- A **detail grid** for transaction-level drill-down and auditing

## Key results

- **$371.19M** total sales tracked, with **23.59% YoY growth**
- **13.26K** units sold year-to-date
- Identified that **SUVs drove ~27%** of YTD revenue, and that two regions (Austin, Scottsdale)
  significantly outperformed the rest of the dealer network — insights that directly informed
  inventory and marketing allocation

## How to explore it

1. Download `Car_sales.pbix`
2. Open in [Power BI Desktop](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/power-platform/products/power-bi/downloads) (free)
3. Use the **Year**, **Body Style**, **Dealer**, **Transmission**, and **Engine** filters to explore
